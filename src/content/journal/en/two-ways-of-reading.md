---
title: One Photo, Two Ways of Reading It
date: 2026-04-18
excerpt: The same photo gets read differently by an engineer and a photographer — trying to write down both readings here.
---

When I showed a friend "The Tree Outside the Ward," I got two completely different reactions. A friend who studied design said the composition was quiet, the light was good. A friend from CS asked whether the dynamic range felt narrow — like the shadow detail had gotten eaten.

Both of them were right. They were just reading the same photo through different coordinate systems.

I've noticed I keep both windows open when I look at my own photos — one feeling, one calibrating. I used to think they'd fight each other. Turns out it's precisely because both are running at once that I pause half a second before the shutter — not just "does this look good," but "can this exposure actually hold the shadow I want."

This one's an attempt to write both readings down, and a reminder to myself that I don't have to pick a side.
