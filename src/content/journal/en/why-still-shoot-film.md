---
title: Why Someone From an Informatics Track Still Shoots Film
date: 2026-06-02
excerpt: A note to myself — slowing down is about seeing more clearly, not wasting time.
---

I studied informatics within biomedical engineering — writing code, running data, debugging past midnight was just daily life. When I first picked up film, people around me thought it was strange: you're someone who's used to seeing results instantly, so why choose a medium where you wait three days to find out if anything worked.

Eventually I realized the two aren't as different as I assumed. When I write code, I rarely get it right the first time — the point was never getting it right immediately, it's that every failure tells me something about the system. Film works the same way. Every roll that comes back, good or bad, tells me something about what I was actually thinking at the time.

The difference is mostly in timing: code gives you feedback instantly, film gives it to you late. And that delay is exactly what forces me to be a little more honest the moment I press the shutter — I can't check a screen to confirm anything, I just have to trust the read I made in that instant.

This one's mostly a note to myself. Slowing down is about seeing more clearly, not wasting time.
